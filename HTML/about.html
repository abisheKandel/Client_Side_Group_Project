About this HealthCare+ prototype

HealthCare+ is a student-built prototype that explores how digital tools could help people find and book NHS appointments with shorter waiting times while avoiding unsafe use of emergency services.

For NHS patients Decision-support, not diagnosis Built for learning & research

At a glance

  • Audience: People trying to decide where to seek NHS care.
  • Main goal: Reduce unnecessary waiting by showing faster options.
  • Scope: Prototype only – no live NHS data or real bookings.

The problem

Patients often face long waiting times and are unsure which NHS service to use for their symptoms. This can lead to:

  • Missed or delayed care for people who need it sooner.
  • Unnecessary pressure on A&E and urgent services.
  • Frustration and confusion when booking appointments.

Who this is for

HealthCare+ is designed for everyday NHS users who:

  • Have non-emergency symptoms and want routine care.
  • Are unsure whether to call 999, 111, or book a GP appointment.
  • Want a simple overview of nearby options and typical waits.

The aim

The aim of this prototype is to simulate a decision-support tool that:

  • Helps users check for obvious emergency warning signs.
  • Lists nearby NHS services and orders them by estimated waiting time.
  • Lets users save and manage basic appointment information in one place.

How it works

  • Assessment: short questionnaire to highlight possible emergencies and direct users towards 999 or NHS 111 when needed.
  • Services: local list of NHS-type providers with distance and simple estimated wait times, filtered by radius and sorted by shortest wait.
  • Appointments: a personal list where users can add, edit, and delete appointments linked to a chosen service.

Technical overview

  • Frontend built with HTML5, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Bootstrap used for layout, responsiveness, and base styling.
  • jQuery used for simple DOM interactions and event handling.
  • Browser localStorage used to simulate saving appointments, chosen services, assessment results, and feedback on the user’s device.

Limitations & future improvements

This is not an official NHS service and has several important limitations:

  • No connection to real NHS systems or live waiting-time data.
  • Does not replace professional triage or clinical judgement.
  • All information is stored only in the browser, not suitable for real patient data.

Future versions could:

  • Integrate with real NHS APIs and data feeds for accurate waiting-time predictions.
  • Use stronger authentication and secure storage for clinical information.
  • Explore smarter routing to reduce waits across different patient groups.
